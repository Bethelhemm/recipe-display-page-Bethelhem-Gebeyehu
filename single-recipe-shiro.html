Shiro

Shiro Wot is a staple in any Ethiopian vegetarian platter - a thick, chickpea stew simmered slowly and served over their traditional sour flatbread, injera, with a host of other vegetarian dishes.

prep time

30 min.

cook time

15 min.

serving

4 servings

instructions

step 1

Heat the oil in a large pan or pot over medium heat.

step 2

Add the chopped onions to the pan and sauté until they become soft and translucent.

step 3

Add the minced garlic to the pan and sauté for another minute until fragrant.

step 4

Add the berbere spice (and any additional spices you prefer) to the pan and stir well to combine with the onions and garlic. Allow the spices to cook for a minute or two to release their flavors.

step 5

Gradually add the shiro powder to the pan while stirring continuously to avoid lumps from forming. Mix well until the shiro powder is fully incorporated with the onion and spice mixture.

step 6

Slowly pour in the vegetable broth or water while stirring constantly. Continue stirring until the mixture thickens to a stew-like consistency. You may need to adjust the amount of liquid depending on the desired thickness of your shiro wot.

step 7

Reduce the heat to low and let the shiro wot simmer for about 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally to prevent sticking and to allow the flavors to meld together. Add more liquid if needed to maintain the desired consistency.

step 8

Taste the shiro wot and season with salt according to your preference.

step 9

Remove from heat and let it rest for a few minutes before serving.

ingredients

Oil - ½ cup

Chickpea flour (Shiro) - ½ cup

Finely chopped onions - 2 medium size

Pureed Tomato - 1

Water - 3 cups

Salt

2 cloves of garlic, minced

tools

Large Heavy Pot With Lid

Measuring Spoons

Measuring Cups
