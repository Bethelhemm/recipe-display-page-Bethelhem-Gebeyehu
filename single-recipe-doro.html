Doro Wot

Doro Wot is an Ethiopian chicken stew flavored with a spiced butter called niter kibbeh and berbere, a spice blend that often includes fenugreek, hot chiles, paprika, cardamom, and coriander. Toward the end of cooking, hard-boiled eggs are added to the stew. Doro wat is typically served with injera bread, a flatbread made with teff, the staple grain of Ethiopia. It's one of the most recognizable Ethiopian foods.

prep time

30 min.

cook time

2 hr.

serving

4 servings

instructions

step 1

Heat oil in a large pot over medium heat.

step 2

Add the chopped onions to the pot and saut√© until they become soft and golden brown. Stir occasionally to prevent burning.

step 3

Add the minced garlic and grated ginger to the pot and cook for another minute until fragrant.

step 4

In a separate bowl, combine the berbere spice, paprika, turmeric, and ground cardamom. Mix well.

step 5

Add the spice mixture to the pot and stir it into the onion mixture. Allow the spices to cook for a couple of minutes to release their flavors.

step 6

Add the chicken pieces to the pot and coat them well with the spice and onion mixture. Cook the chicken for a few minutes, turning occasionally, until it starts to brown.

step 7

Pour in the chicken broth or water, ensuring that the chicken is fully submerged. Bring the mixture to a simmer.

step 8

Reduce the heat to low and cover the pot. Let the doro wot simmer for about 60-90 minutes, or until the chicken is tender and cooked through. Stir occasionally and add more liquid if needed.

step 9

If desired, add the lemon juice to the pot for added tanginess. Adjust the salt to taste.

step 10

Add hard-boiled eggs to the doro wot during the last 15-20 minutes of cooking. The eggs will absorb the flavors of the stew.

step 11

Remove from heat and let the doro wot rest for a few minutes before serving.

ingredients

2-3 pounds chicken pieces (drumsticks, thighs, or a combination)

3 large onions, finely chopped

4 cloves of garlic, minced

1 tablespoon grated ginger

1/4 cup niter kibbeh (spiced clarified butter)

2 tablespoons berbere spice (adjust to your preferred level of spiciness)

1 teaspoon paprika

1/2 teaspoon turmeric

1/4 teaspoon ground cardamom

Salt

1 cup of water

2 tablespoons lemon juice (optional)

Hard-boiled eggs

tools

Large pot

Cutting Board and Knife

Mixing bowl

Measuring Spoons

Measuring Cups
