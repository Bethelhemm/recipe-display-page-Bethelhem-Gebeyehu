Butcha

Butcha is a flavorful Ethiopian dip or spread made with either fava bean flour or chickpea flour combined with olive oil, water, onions, mustard, lemon juice, and hot pepper flakes.

prep time

30 min.

cook time

15 min.

serving

4 servings

instructions

step 1

Add water and olive oil into a pan and bring it to a low boil, then stir in the chickpea flour.

step 2

When the mixture begins to heat and bubble, add the ginger, lemon juice, and turmeric. Loosely place some aluminum foil over the top to keep the mix from splattering. But DO NOT put a lid on it – the water needs to slowly boil away for the mix to thicken properly. Notice that the volume will decrease as it cooks.

step 3

Let the mixture cook for 20 minutes or so. When the mixture starts to thicken, remove the pot from heat and pour it into a skillet.

step 4

On the skillet, heat the mixture and stir it constantly for 10 -15 min. until it begins to slightly dry out. When the mixture is thick enough, remove from heat and pour it into a bowl and place it in the fridge for about 1 hr.

step 5

When it’s time to serve the Buticha, add the seeded and finely chopped onions and thoroughly stir them all together.

ingredients

6 tbsp chickpea flour

2 tbsp olive oil

2 cups of water

¼ tsp turmeric powder

¼ cup of onions, finely chopped

tools

Hand Blender

Large Heavy Pot With Lid

Measuring Spoons

Measuring Cups
